## Problem Space

The user wants a small, deliberate set of original sci-fi poems on the theme of Artificial Intelligence. The three pieces are designed as a **tonal trio** — one funny, one weird, one dark — so that the set as a whole shows range rather than hammering a single mood. This is a creative-writing deliverable, not a technical project; the goal is finished poems that read well.

## Validated Goals

### Goal 1: Three original sci-fi poems about AI

**Description**: Produce three distinct, original poems. Each is sci-fi in setting/imagery and centers on Artificial Intelligence as its subject.

**Rationale**: The user asked for exactly three poems on this theme. Three is enough to demonstrate range without bloating into a chapbook.

**Key considerations**: Poems must be original (no pastiche of recognizable existing works). Sci-fi framing should be present but not gimmicky — the AI subject carries the piece, the genre supplies the imagery.

### Goal 2: Tonal trio — funny / weird / dark

**Description**: The three poems are differentiated by tone: one funny, one weird, one dark. The set is unified by subject and genre, but each piece occupies a different emotional register.

**Rationale**: The user explicitly chose this contrast over a connected narrative arc. The tonal split gives the trio cohesion (it's clearly a set) without forcing a sequence.

**Key considerations**: Each tone should be committed to — funny means actually funny, dark means actually dark, weird means genuinely strange. Avoid hedged/half-tones that read as all the same poem in three costumes.

### Goal 3: Stand on their own as finished pieces

**Description**: Each poem should be readable and complete on its own, not a fragment or sketch. The set is the deliverable — three polished poems, ready as-is.

**Rationale**: The brainstorm produced no requirement for illustration, performance, or further development. The poems themselves are the artifact.

**Key considerations**: "Finished" means edited, not just drafted — line breaks, word choice, and ending lines should be deliberate.

## Scope Boundaries

### In Scope
- Three original poems
- Sci-fi imagery/setting
- AI as the subject of each poem
- One poem per tone: funny, weird, dark
- Poems delivered as text in a single document

### Out of Scope
- A connecting narrative arc across the three poems
- A shared world, recurring character, or recurring motif across the set (unless one emerges naturally)
- Illustration, audio, or performance treatment
- Form/length constraints (haiku, sonnet, page count) — open-ended, each poem finds its own length and form
- Additional poems beyond the three (a fourth, alternates, drafts)
- Any non-AI sci-fi themes (aliens, space travel, time travel) unless they serve the AI focus

## Key Constraints

- **Subject discipline**: every poem is *about* AI, not just set in a world that has AI. AI must be the central concern of each piece.
- **Tonal commitment**: the three tones are non-negotiable role assignments — one poem per tone, no overlap.
- **Originality**: poems are written from scratch, not derived from or imitative of identifiable existing works.

## Open Questions

- Form, length, and meter of each poem are deliberately left open for the planner/writer to decide based on what serves each tone best.
- Voice/perspective (first-person AI, human observer, omniscient) is left open — may vary per poem to reinforce tonal contrast.
- No shared world or recurring motif is required, but the writer may introduce one if it strengthens the set without compromising the tonal split.

## Summary

POEMS-1 produces three original sci-fi poems about Artificial Intelligence, deliberately split across three tones — funny, weird, and dark — so the set shows range. There is no required narrative arc or shared world; cohesion comes from subject (AI), genre (sci-fi), and the intentional tonal contrast. Form, length, and voice are left to the writer's discretion per poem. The deliverable is three finished, polished pieces in a single document.
