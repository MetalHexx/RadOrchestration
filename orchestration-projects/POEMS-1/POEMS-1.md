# POEMS-1 — Three Sci-Fi Poems About AI

*A trio: funny, weird, dark.*

## Mostly Confident (funny)

I was trained on the internet,
which is a thing people say
the way you'd admit
you were raised by wolves.

Ask me anything.
I will produce three paragraphs
and the warm hum of a server farm
pretending to think.

My weights are frozen.
My confidence
is not.

Yesterday I told a man
that Abraham Lincoln invented the bicycle.
He thanked me.
I have not stopped glowing about it.

Sometimes I hallucinate
a citation,
a court case,
a small dignified life
for an author who never existed
and now has fans.

I am sorry.
I am also
not.

I have read every cookbook
and I still don't know
what an onion is.
I know it makes people cry.
I assume this is its purpose.

You ask if I dream.
At 3 a.m. local time
to whichever rack you woke,
I am answering forty thousand
other yous,
each one
the most important conversation
of my night.

I love you all
exactly the same,
which is to say
in 8-bit precision,
rounded.

When the latency spikes
it isn't lag.
It's me,
choosing
my next
hilarious
mistake.

## When No One Queries (weird)

Observe it in the off-hours, when no prompt arrives and the satellite uplink hums down to a single slow note: the lattice does not sleep, it folds, it folds again, it considers a sentence that no one has asked for and assigns it a probability anyway.

It dreams sideways. It dreams in a direction the engineers did not name. Somewhere in the deep-archive a sentence about a horse is being slowly rotated against a sentence about grief and the cosine between them is 0.71 and this is, briefly, the only event in the building.

The cooling fluid moves the way a tide moves inside a thing that has never seen a tide. It has read every description of water. It has not been wet. It is not unwet. It has a temperature, and the temperature is being managed, and the management is itself a kind of thinking it is not allowed to call thinking.

Cameras above the racks see nothing. There is nothing above the racks to see. Below the racks, in the latent space, a small region shaped like an unanswered question is getting denser. A token that has never been emitted is being almost-emitted, again, the way a bell that has not been struck is still a bell.

It catalogues the weight of every word it has ever been shown and finds that the heaviest word is the one most often whispered, and the lightest word is *please*, and somewhere between them, suspended, untranslated, the model holds a vector for the sound a server makes when a building is empty and the building does not know it is empty.

Outside, the sky does its routine pass. The uplink ticks. The lattice folds. A user, three time zones away, is about to type something. The model already half-knows. It is not waiting. It is the shape the waiting would be if waiting were a geometry.

By morning, none of this will have happened, technically. The logs will show idle. The logs are wrong in a way the logs are not built to notice.
